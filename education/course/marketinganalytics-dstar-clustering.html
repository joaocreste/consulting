02 · Findings

Four archetypes. One priority list.

The estate splits cleanly along two axes: commercial throughput (PC1, 44% of variance) and distribution depth (PC2, 14%). What follows maps every store, names each cluster, and ranks the ten that earn premium rep attention.

F · 01
Premium on-trade is a small, high-leverage tier.
Six stores form Cluster 1 — bars, hotel bars and restaurants with sellout values 2 standard deviations above the mean. Five of the six earn a top-10 ranking on composite score. Concentrated rep attention here protects the bulk of revenue.
F · 02
Distribution depth lives elsewhere.
The deepest PR shelf footprints (10 brands, 25+ SKUs) are not in Premium On-Trade but in specialist off-licences and convenience stores — S39, S10, S32. These stores are commercial mid-tier but strategic upper-tier for range protection.
F · 03
Mid-tier on-trade is the obvious upgrade pool.
Cluster 2 (n=9) sits one notch below the premium tier on revenue but matches it on distribution depth. With targeted activation investment — cocktail menu listings, premium pour visibility — these are the candidates most likely to migrate into the top tier next cycle.
F · 04
The long tail is the rep-visit efficiency question.
Fifteen stores cluster in the low-velocity tail (Cluster 3) — half of the estate. They average £200/mo sellout against current rep visit schedules. Worth a service-model review: less frequent visits, distributor-led replenishment, or tier-down decisions.

09 · Methodology

How the model was built.

Standard practice, deliberately conservative. Decisions are documented so the analysis can be reproduced or contested.

STEP · 01
Feature preparation
Ten numeric variables z-scored (zero mean, unit variance) so no single metric dominates by scale. Channel type one-hot encoded and ×0.5 downweighted — informative but not dominant.
STEP · 02
k selection
K-means tested at k = 3, 4, 5. Silhouette scores were close (0.15 / 0.16 / 0.17). k = 4 chosen for interpretability — three collapses the on-trade tiers; five fragments the long tail without actionable separation.
STEP · 03
PCA projection
Two-component PCA for visualisation only. PC1 captures throughput (sellout, rep visits, density); PC2 captures distribution depth (PR brands carried, SKUs). 58% of variance explained in 2D — adequate for cluster validation.
STEP · 04
Composite score
Five strategic dimensions, min-max scaled to 0–100 across the estate, weighted at 35/25/20/10/10. Weights are advisory not algorithmic — a commercial director can shift them and re-rank. Score is a decision aid, not a verdict.
